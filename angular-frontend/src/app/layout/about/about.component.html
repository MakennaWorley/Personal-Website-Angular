A Little About Me

I’m a senior at Westminster University (Class of 2026) studying Computer Science with minors in Data Science and Mathematics. I’m part of the Honors College, a member of the First Scholars program as a first-generation college student, and I’ve been on the Dean’s List throughout college.

My work focuses on building data-driven systems that automate complex workflows and turn raw information into practical tools. I currently work as a Data & Analytics Intern at Ken Garff Automotive, where I develop full-stack applications, design REST APIs, and build internal tools that support operational and data workflows. Previously, I worked as a Software Developer Intern at RanLife Home Loans, where I supported production systems and contributed to internal development projects.

Outside of internships, I’ve worked as a tutor in Westminster’s Mathematics, Computer Science, and Physics Tutoring Center, served as an undergraduate researcher on multiple faculty-led projects, and volunteer as a chess instructor and co-director for the elementary school chess club where I first learned to play. Whether I’m building software, teaching students, or working on research, I’m most interested in creating tools that make complex systems easier to understand and use.
